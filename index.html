The Challenge
Two Crises. One Solution.

The Burning Problem

Every harvest season, millions of tons of crop residue are burned because removal is expensive and time-sensitive. The result:

  • Catastrophic air quality in India
  • PM2.5 spikes 10× above safe limits
  • Soil degradation from repeated burning
  • No economic incentive to stop

The AgriLoop Solution

We turn residue into revenue. Farmers deposit waste into our nearby hubs and get paid per kilogram. The hubs do the rest:

  • Mycelium grows on biomass in 5–7 days
  • IoT sensors guarantee batch quality
  • Molded into biodegradable packaging
  • Zero landfill. Zero burn. Full circle.
The Process
From Field to Packaging in 7 Days
01

Farmer Deposits Residue

Farmers bring paddy straw, wheat stubble, or husk to the nearest AgriLoop hub. They are paid ₹2–4 per kg, creating a direct income stream.

02

IoT-Monitored Fungal Growth

Mycelium is seeded onto the prepared biomass. Sensors track temperature (22–26°C), humidity (70–85%), and CO₂ across every tray, every hour.

03

Molding & Quality Control

After 5–7 days, the colonized biomass is heat-pressed into product molds — corner guards, cushions, trays, insulation panels.

04

Delivered to B2B Clients

Products ship to e-commerce firms, electronics brands, and FMCG companies as certified biodegradable replacements for thermocol and foam.
